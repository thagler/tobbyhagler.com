Making Things That Take the Time They Take

Some things come together fast. Other things take their sweet time. There's no way to know until you commit to writing it.

I wrote "Sin-Eater Claws" for Kozy Krampus in a single weekend. When it showed up, it was ready to go. But then one of my favorite flash pieces, "So: No Fix," has been stuck on the struggle bus for months.

They're given the same love, the same levels of inspiration, and they share the same desire to tell the story. They just come at very different speeds.

I'm not exactly sure why this is. It's not writer's block -- that dreadful experience of not knowing the characters or story well enough to tell it yet. Instead, it's this inexplicable feeling of "why the Hell isn't this done yet?"

I’m learning to stop rushing the slow ones or doubting the fast ones. If a story needs more time, that doesn’t mean it’s broken. It just isn’t done yet. And just because it comes to life in a day doesn't mean it's half-cocked.

The thing I'm constantly trying to remind myself: "Things take the time they take. Let them."
