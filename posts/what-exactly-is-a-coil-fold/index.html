What Exactly Is a Coil Fold?

A coil fold is a gentle way to strengthen dough during bulk fermentation without kneading the life out of it. Instead of pushing or punching, you pick the dough up and let gluten align itself. It’s especially great for high-hydration doughs that would rather pretend to be soup.

How to do it:

  1. Wet your hands (dough respects hydration diplomacy).
  2. Slide both hands under the center of the dough.
  3. Lift until the dough stretches under its own weight.
  4. Tuck the middle underneath so the dough folds over itself.
  5. Rotate the bowl 90° and repeat. Usually, four folds equals one set.

What you’re looking for:

  • First set: dough still acts like a puddle
  • Second set: starting to hold shape after the fold
  • Third set: smoother surface, a little resistance
  • Fourth set: it’s a dough now, not a blob

Why it works (the science bit):

  • Aligns gluten strands instead of tearing them.
  • Traps gas in the network yeast is building.
  • Builds surface tension that helps it rise up, not out.

This whole technique is basically teaching the dough to sit up straight.
