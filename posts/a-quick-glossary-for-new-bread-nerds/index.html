A Quick Glossary for New Bread Nerds

I still find myself forgetting baking terminology. I'm starting a glossary to keep myself sane(-ish).

Autolyse
Flour + water rest before adding starter/salt. Enzymes start gluten and flavor development while you do nothing. A miracle.

Bulk Fermentation (Bulk)
First rise as one dough. Flavor and structure are built here.

Proof / Final Proof
Second rise after shaping. A good proof = "confident loaf."

Surface Tension
Tight outer “skin” of the dough that holds gas in and pushes the loaf upward. Shaping and coil folds build it. Don't confuse this for the dry crust that forms when it's cold-proofed without a proper cover.

Slack Dough
Dough that spreads like it has no ambitions. High hydration or weak gluten. Needs gentleness, not judgment.

Oven Spring
That dramatic lift in the first 10 minutes of baking. The loaf’s final party before becoming food.

Starter
Your living jar of wild yeast + bacteria, fed daily-ish. The thing I treat like a bread pet, with a name and everything.

Levain
A separate build from the starter for that bake. Gives you control over flavor, timing, and strength.
Every levain is a starter, but not every starter is a levain.

Tool and Technique Terms

Bassinage
Adding water after mixing to gradually increase hydration. Helps gluten develop without making early mixing a slip-n-slide.
"Learn to hold yourself before holding more water."

Banneton (the bowl thingie)
The woven proofing basket that helps dough keep its shape and gives it those cool swirl flour patterns.

Bench Knife / Dough Scraper
Your best friend. Cuts dough cleanly and scoops it with dignity. Much easier to use than fingers, especially with a sticky autolyse. 

Scoring
Intentional slicing right before baking so the loaf opens where you choose — not wherever it feels dramatic.

Coil Fold
Lifting dough from the center and tucking it under itself to build gluten with minimum violence.

Bonus Terms That Come Up a Lot

Autolyze vs. Autolyse
Same thing. Welcome to bread spelling chaos.

Gluten Development
How strong and stretchy the dough is.
Built with folds + time, not elbow grease. Kneading won't help.

Degassing
Gently pressing out some gas so that a new structure can form without huge bubbles taking over, like middle-school group projects.

Scoring
Slicing the top of the loaf right before baking.
Creates a planned expansion zone so the loaf doesn’t rip itself open like Hulk's jeans.

Crumb
The inside texture of baked bread.
Open crumb = large, airy holes.
Tight crumb = smooth sandwich slice energy.

Shape Words I’ll Actually Use

Batard
An oval loaf. The middle ground between baguette and boule. Excellent for sandwiches and show-off breads.

Boule
An oval loaf. The middle ground between baguette and boule. Excellent for sandwiches and confidence." 

Baguette
Long loaf. More difficult than Instagram leads you to believe. IYKYK.

Focaccia
Flatbread drenched in olive oil and happiness. The oil fixes ALL mistakes. OMG! SO MUCH olive oil. Just drown it.

A Diagnostic Cheat Sheet

If someone says…

  1. “It needs more structure”: more folds / more time in bulk
  2. “It’s overproofed”: gluten can’t hold gas anymore
  3. “It’s underproofed”: dough hasn’t built enough gas yet
  4. “It’s slack”: support it with shaping + tension

If you say…

  • “What the hell is happening?”: probably hydration or temperature
