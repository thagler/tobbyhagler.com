A Note on Keeping Two Projects Alive

People sometimes ask how I juggle multiple writing projects, and the answer is, "badly, but with commitment." I’ve learned that when one story is being stubborn, the best thing I can do is let it sulk in a corner until it comes to its senses and decides to let me work it again. It turns out jealousy and FOMO are powerful creative motivators.

Keeping at least two things alive at once keeps me out of the existential gooey-center of “I must make progress on the 'One Big Thing™' or I am failing at life somehow.” Instead, I can rotate projects like a lazy susan of narrative chaos. If characters from my latest novel aren't cooperating, I can go talk to the goblins. If the goblins are quiet (they rarely are quiet, but they do shut me out frequently), I can work on a flash fiction piece for a contest or an open call with a specific prompt.

So, what’s currently breathing in the ole brainpan?

  • The Grove sequel
    Most of the crew is back, and the stakes are galactically existential. Because of course they are.

  • Frankie and the Goblin King
    Frankie’s leg is fine, but his patience with the deposed goblin king who inhabits one? Contested even on the best of days.

  • A cluster of short stories + flash
    They’re out there in the world trying to get adopted by various lit mags. Wish them luck.

  • Station and Gary
    Station built Gary because it was lonely. But Gary is an asshole and now Station has two problems.

Keeping multiple projects alive isn’t multitasking; it’s survival. When one story is stuck in cold fermentation, another one usually starts to rise. And I don’t need everything to bake at once. I just need to make sure nothing dies in the back of the mental fridge.
