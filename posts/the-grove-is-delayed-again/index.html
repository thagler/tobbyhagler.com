The Grove is Delayed Again

...But that's actually a good thing.

I've been a writer for years, and The Grove of Tau Ceti is my first (finished) novel. I'm really happy with it, too. It took a long time to get here, but I'm now one click away from being a published sci-fi author. I even have physical copies with fantastic cover art by Nik Wilets.

With the help of JB Kish and his Writing Milestones coaching, I could see the path from writer to author. Publication day was initially May 23. With a deadline set—and nothing motivates me more than beating a deadline—we crafted the complete plan I needed to write and publish a novel.

For every milestone reached, I loved the story and its characters even more (except Hector). So when the manuscript came back from my editor, I sent query letters to a few literary agents. You know... because why not?

My stack of rejection letters grew fast. And that's okay. Rejection letters mean you're putting in the work. Right?

Then I got a few of the kind that say, "I love the premise, but it's not what I'm looking for right now." That's still a no, but at least it's an encouraging no. So I kept at it.

And then one day... "Would you mind sending along the next NN pages/chapters?" 

As May 23 approached, my manuscript was still under consideration. I pushed the self-publish date to July 25 to leave the door open for traditional publishing possibilities, while keeping a publish-by date on the counter to keep me accountable.

After months of silence, I assumed it wasn't in the stars. It's cool, though. The plan was always to self-publish. Finding a few interested agents who ultimately send rejections still exceeded my expectations when I began working with JB.

And that leads up to why I'm delaying it again. Agents are hellabusy, and sometimes they just can't get back to you for a long time. And I recently got a "still currently considering the project" update, which... [insert Carlton Dance meme]!

I want the best possible publication for The Grove of Tau Ceti, which is why I've decided to postpone the launch again.

My apologies to friends and family who frequently ask, "Is your book out yet?" and I've replied with dates that have come and gone. For now, that answer is simply: "Soon!"
