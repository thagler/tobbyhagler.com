What I’m Trying to Get Better At Right Now

Here are a few things I'm actively trying to improve with my craft.

  • Give better feedback
    I love getting feedback, even when it burns a little. Giving feedback, though? Hard af. I want to get better at two things:
    1. Spotting what another writer’s story truly needs (what works, what doesn't, and why), and
    2. Delivering that feedback in a constructive way that helps the story, not just critiques the draft.

  • Finishing things before I start new things
    New ideas are shiny. Endings take work; don't give up on them.

  • Stop sharpening my pencils
    I prefer outlining to pantsing. And that's perfectly fine. But it does make getting started tough; I have outlined some stories to the Nth degree and never started. It frequently leads to bad cases of Blank Page Syndrome. 

  • Trusting the slow stories
    Not every draft arrives ready to sprint to the finish line. Keep revisiting them, even if I'm working on something else. If the idea won't leave my head, or the characters won't stop nagging me, trust that there's probably something worth writing.

  • Keeping characters consistent under stress
    Fear and high stakes shouldn’t erase their personality. Don't force them to act, just to move the plot forward.

  • Telling myself, “good enough for today.”
    Perfect is the enemy of good. Get the words on paper while they're still in my brain. I can fix them later.

  • Not apologizing for writing time
    It's okay to write. I don't "need" permission. I shouldn't feel guilty for writing.
