The Grove of Tau Ceti

Demeter's not herself anymore.

Once the pride of humanity’s interstellar fleet, the ESS Demeter is on a final voyage to Tau Ceti, where the promise of a new alliance awaits. But something stirs deep within the ship’s rootwork, hidden and ancient, unfolding.

When psychic interference from a nearby alien moon plunges Demeter into strange silence, an unsettling truth emerges: the ship’s organic systems may no longer be under their control.

A telepathic technician haunted by her past must reawaken her abilities to help save Demeter. With the aid of a fellow Druid, a disgraced engineer, and a sentient sapling, the crew fights for survival against an unknown intelligence bent on reshaping them.

With time running out, the remaining crew face an impossible choice: reclaim their home or risk losing the galaxy.

Kozy Krampus*

We arrive yet again at the end of it all: the dreaded holiday season, where everything is too bright, nothing is where it should be, and you will be constantly hounded by the persistent dread of having forgotten something very important. And traffic will be totally and utterly f*cked all the way to the mall. We are doomed. 

But there may still be some spark in these fading minutes before the heat death of the universe, before the last tear is shed, and before the last present is unwrapped. Madness awaits, surely, but there may still be light to guide us to a distant haven. Come with us, once again, across this desolate doomscape of humanity's own creation.  

Kozy Krampus (Underland Press) contains 29 stories* of the nocturnal monstrosities that slither down our chimneys while the coals cool and quiet, of the awkward terrors that lurk within pretty boxes with prettier bows, of the doom that creeps into our bowels as we cram yet another slice of sticky, gummy, oh-what-the-hell-is-this? fruitcake into our mouths.

These are the horrors visited upon us by family, festivities, and futility. You will get the wrong gift for someone who cares too deeply about material things. You will recoil too often from someone who merely wants to snuggle during the winter months. You will wait up all night for someone to come save you, knowing full well that they will never arrive. 

Welcome to the cozy cosmic horror of the holiday season. 

Edited by Mark Teppo & Frances Lu-Pai Ippolito

* Tobby Hagler is no. 19 on the list of stories.
